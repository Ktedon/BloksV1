About


Bloks is social media site for the Essex-Westford School District. Bloks is a democratic, student run, and principled site designed to fulfill the needs of the students of the Essex-Westford School District and to protect student digital privacy and rights. Only students with a @@vt.ewsd.org school email can create an account, this is to protect students for foreign influence and to ensure that students can only create one account.



Bloks is developed and maintained by Bradly Ovitt will generous support and involvement from the EHS student council and the Bloks Government.


Bloks Student Manifesto:


According to the Census Bureau, in Vermont, 9.8% of children live below the poverty line with those living in poverty representing 33.4% of african american vermonters, 14.3% of asian american vermonters, 17.9% of latino vermonters, and 9.5% of caucasian vermonters. Similarly the percentage of disconnected youth is at 8%. The teen birth rate is 8.8 per 1000. Health insurance only exists for 6.8% of the population. The number of children living away from their parents is 11 per 1000. Hunger and food insecurity is at 9.6%.

We should not doubt that day by day, hour by hour, and minute by minute, each of these children in unfavorable situations fade a bit more. In debates politicians talk about us, around us … on us. They use us as an issue while we struggle and are already silently being forgotten. How are we supposed to save ourselves as ghastly virtual human beings only capable of being represented by numbers and statistics?

As children we are forced to depend on adults and the conditions we are forced to live in are a reflection of how we are treated and to what degree we are respected. We cannot thrive without understanding our own place, unfortunately, our place is denuded, ravaged, and destroyed by one insult or another populist argument for war. We must fight back to achieve palpable existence. We have to save ourselves from being columns of numbers and percentages, bar graphs and bell curves. We must force our leaders to see, hear, smell, and fear us and all that we stand for: Their own futures.

With all the activists and senators working on our behalf. They have failed us. Today’s children are faced with global warming, cyber-security issues, mental health issues, under-funding of the educational system, social inequities, etc. Current leaders are failing to meet our needs for all of these issues. In the start of our lives and careers. We are facing a hypercompetitive market for employment and success which we have not been properly prepared to succeed in. The reason for this is simple: We lack representation. In our world we are denied our unalienable rights. We do not have the right to bear arms, We do not have the right to land, liberty, or property, we cannot vote, we cannot learn to drive without permission, we cannot choose our classes without permission. And in this way, we do not have the right to choose our future without permission. We are not given the rights designed to prevent government abuses. Minor’s are America's secretly marginalized group. We are refused the freedoms to take care of ourselves and refused the resources to be safe. We are at the complete mercy of our leaders.

Whenever politicians use us to make empty promises about a brighter future, it is not for us. It is never for us because we have no power. It is for them. They pass us through these indoctrination facilities that tell us to believe in their ideas, the very ideas that gave us the issues we face today. They refuse to give us the resources we need to be successful and insist it is for our own good. I call BS.

They call us moody, irrational, and impulsive. They say we are incapable of making proper decisions for ourselves, meanwhile they replace our future with an overfunded military industrial complex, extreme wealth inequality, and an obesity epidemic. They beat us senseless then tell us that putting our hand up is an act of indecent defiance.

We should demand our rights. The rights we humans should always have been granted and the rights we were promised in the declaration of independence. Bloks can give us a chance. More than a chance, a fight. It’s time we act. It’s time we unite for a brighter future.

- Bradly Ovitt, General Secretary of the Blok Union
